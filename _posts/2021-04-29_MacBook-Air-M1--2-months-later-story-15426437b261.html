Good with some limitations

It has been 2-months since I have purchased the lowest model MacBook Air M1. So, let me discuss the pros and cons of it.

Pros

It insanely fast for a web developer like myself. I am a full stack developer, and this machine works well in PHP, JavaScript (Node.js, React) development.

Battery life is a game-changer even with Parallel Desktop workloads. Recently I can leave a heavy 30W charger at home and charge it every night. But do not get me wrong. I still keep my small 18W charger for a power-hungry workload.

Cons

There are severe printer and scanner issues in macOS. I thought this is a driver-related issue. It affects only some old printers and scanners. Fortunately, there is a workaround, which is to print or scan via Parallel Desktop.

It has a thermal throttle problem, but it not a big deal for me. My main concern is about a cooldown time. Without any active cooling fan, it can take more than 15 minutes to cool it down. Oh, I almost forget, the thermal throttle makes this notebook not stable enough for a long-time streaming application like OBS.

At the time of writing this story, Parallel Desktop is supporting only ARM and x86 architecture. Thus, I still do not recommend this machine for advanced workload in Windows.

Limited port, as this machine has only 2-ports. I have to carry multiple ports hub, especially in this Zoom-meeting era because I have an external webcam and microphone.

That is it, what I have learned from using this machine in the past 2-months. Again, this is a good machine but not great because there are some limitations.
