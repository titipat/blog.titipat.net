My second impression of AppleÂ Silicon

They work perfectly fine for every usage, but not for virtualization and emulation workloads.

I have brought Mac Mini M1 since the first day of release. There are massive compatible issues at that moment. So, I have returned it.

Then three months passed, I did research again. I have found that most of my tools can work perfectly fine right now. Some of them even got a native Apple Silicon optimization. So, I have decided to buy MacBook Air M1 to replace my 16-inch MacBook Pro for web developing tasks. Thus, it works well for me, and I have very satisfied.

Unfortunately, some solutions still can not work in Apple Silicon. For instance, GNS3 0.8.7, a network simulation software I used in network engineering class. In macOS, the program is installable, but it freezes when trying to add device images. Then, I try again in Window 10 ARM insider preview and Parallel Desktop preview. At this time, I can add the device images, but some of the dependencies are not compatible and instantly crash after launch. My last resort is Ubuntu 20.04 ARM version, but again there are dependency software issues.

To conclude, at this writing time (March 2021), Apple Silicon works well for everyday usages. However, I still do not recommend Apple Silicon for virtualization and emulation workloads at this moment. So, I will keep my heavy 16-inch MacBook Pro Intel-based for this kind of workload.
